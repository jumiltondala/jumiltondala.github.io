Monexa

Monexa is a real P2P currency exchange project operating between Angola and South Africa (AOA ↔ ZAR).

It was created from direct operational experience with informal exchanges, real clients, and real financial friction.

The Problem

Cross-border money transfers between Angola and South Africa are often slow, expensive, and inaccessible for many individuals.

While informal exchange networks exist, they usually lack structure, transparency, and proper risk control, creating uncertainty for both sides.

How Monexa Works

Monexa operates as an intermediary between currency suppliers and end clients.

Currency is sourced from trusted suppliers at negotiated rates and exchanged with clients using controlled margins. Each transaction is manually verified and tracked.

Business Model

Revenue is generated through the spread between buy and sell rates. The focus is on consistency, volume, and operational discipline rather than speculation.

Why It Matters

Monexa reduces friction for individuals who rely on cross-border payments for daily needs, not investment.

Trust, clarity, and operational control are treated as core assets of the system.

Current Stage

Monexa is currently operating manually using structured controls built in Google Sheets, allowing full visibility over transactions, margins, and cash flow.

This phase validates the business logic before any automation.

Future Vision

The long-term vision is to evolve Monexa into a fully digital platform with automated workflows, compliance layers, and scalable operations.

Technology will be introduced progressively, without compromising operational clarity.
